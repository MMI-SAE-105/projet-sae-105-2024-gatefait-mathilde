BIOGRAPHY

Matthew Vaughnis a British director, producer, and screenwriter, renowned for his dynamic action films and comic book adaptations.

Born in London in 1971, he began his film career as a producer, collaborating with Guy Ritchie on the hit *Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels* (1998).

This successful partnership continued with *Snatch* (2000), solidifying his position in the film industry.

Known for his unique visual style and inventive action scenes, Vaughn stands out for his ability to blend intensity with humor, creating films that entertain while offering a fresh take on genre conventions.

Matthew Vaughn is a director known for his unique style. Here are five of his most famous films.

His first film, *Layer Cake*, follows a drug dealer (Daniel Craig) looking to leave the criminal underworld. The film is praised for its stylish narrative.

In 2010, adapted from a comic, he tells the story of Dave Lizewski (Aaron Taylor-Johnson), a teenager who becomes a superhero without powers in a film called *Kick-Ass*. The film stands out for its dark humor and stylized violence.

In 2011, he explores the origins of mutants and the relationship between Charles Xavier (James McAvoy) and Erik Lensherr (Michael Fassbender). Acclaimed for its performances and dramatic depth, *X-Men: First Class* was a major success.

Finally, *Kingsman* blends action and comedy, following Eggsy (Taron Egerton), a young man recruited by a spy agency, and a collaboration between the Kingsman and their American counterparts, the Statesman, to combat a new threat. These films are famous for their bold action scenes and humor.

VAUGHN CHOOSES GLASSES

In 2014, Vaughn directed one of his most iconic films: *Kingsman: The Secret Service*.

Adapted from the comic *The Secret Service* by Mark Millar and Dave Gibbons, the film combines action and satire, telling the story of a British secret organization that trains a young man to become an elite agent.

Glasses play a central role in the film, serving as both sophisticated technological gadgets for spies and symbols of refined style.

They enable agents to communicate remotely, access augmented reality, and even hold holographic meetings, illustrating the perfect blend of elegance and technology in the Kingsman universe.
