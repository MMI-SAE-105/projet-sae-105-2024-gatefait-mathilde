Glasses are an optical instrument that revolutionized the world. In this article, we will explore their origins and their future evolution.

Timeline

13th Century

First glasses

Glasses, which appeared in the 13th century, revolutionized our relationship with vision. Initially, they were used primarily to correct visual defects, mainly for monks. Early frames were rudimentary, but the invention of bifocal lenses by Benjamin Franklin in the 18th century marked a significant turning point, making glasses more versatile.

1838

3D glasses

3D glasses, based on stereoscopy invented in 1838, allow viewing in three dimensions by simulating depth perception. Initially, red and blue filters (anaglyphs) were used, but polarized glasses and LCD systems later enhanced the 3D experience. Today, they are essential in cinema, video games, and virtual reality, offering a more realistic immersion.

1968

Virtual reality headset

The virtual reality headset was created in 1968 by Ivan Sutherland. Its commercialization really took off in the 2010s. With technological advancements, glasses have evolved into more immersive devices. The headset completely covers the eyes and offers total immersion in digital worlds, ideal for gaming, training, or even medicine. The concept of extended vision has reached a whole new level.

2013-2021

Google Glasses, Ray-Ban Stories

Google Glass, launched in 2013, was the first connected eyewear with a screen and camera, but it failed commercially. Ray-Ban Stories, released in 2021 in partnership with Meta, can record videos and respond to voice commands while maintaining a discreet design. These glasses showcase advances in new technologies, blending style and digital features for everyday use.

Today

Modern corrective glasses

Glasses, once solely tools for correction, are now also fashion and high-tech accessories. With the development of bifocal and multifocal lenses, including adaptive options that adjust tint based on light, they offer increased comfort. Today, they combine visual correction and personal expression, reflecting each individual's style.

Style Glasses

2050

Mixed reality glasses/headset

Mixed reality glasses could transform our interaction with digital content by replacing traditional computers. By projecting virtual screens into our field of vision, they allow browsing the Internet, checking emails, and collaborating while on the move. This technology could revolutionize industries like education and medicine by offering immersive experiences.

2070

Smart contact lenses

By 3000, we could take the process even further. Contact lenses could use micro-screens, sensors, and miniaturized circuits to provide real-time data while remaining comfortable to wear. They illustrate the future of new technologies, aiming to merge digital content with human vision for medical and augmented reality applications.

3000

Connected implant

A brain implant could soon replace glasses and computers, correcting vision and displaying information directly in our field of vision. Through neural stimulation, users could access the Internet simply by thinking.
